# SA Coparents — Relational Mediation Prep

## What it is

**SA Coparents** is a calm, child-centered preparation tool for parents heading
into a co-parenting mediation session. It helps a parent move from emotionally
reactive to focused, prepared, and child-centered — *before* they sit across the
table from their co-parent and a mediator.

This is not a chat app, a legal-advice product, or a substitute for therapy.
It's a private workspace where one parent at a time can think clearly,
externalize the noise, and arrive at mediation with a written agenda the
mediator can read in two minutes.

The product is live in production at **https://sacoparentsapp.online** and is
designed for San Antonio / Bexar County families, with local hotline and
shelter resources alongside national ones.

---

## Who it's for

- Parents preparing for a relational or court-ordered mediation session
- Survivors of domestic violence considering parallel parenting (the safer
  framework when traditional co-parenting isn't viable)
- Mediators and attorneys who want their clients to arrive *ready* instead of
  reactive — fewer billable hours spent on emotion management

---

## How a parent uses it (the 5-step prep wizard)

1. **Child-Centered Goals** — Select from a curated list (Emotional safety,
   Stability, School success, Healthy relationship with both parents, Reduced
   conflict exposure, Healthy routines, Communication consistency), plus
   reflect on what their child currently needs.
2. **Issues & Concerns** — Quietly name what's actually happening from a
   fixed list of 10 specific concerns (poor communication, badmouthing, plan
   breaches, using the child as a messenger, inconsistent parenting,
   refusing cooperation, exposing the child to adult conflict, unreliability,
   emotional manipulation, child safety) — plus an open "anything else" field.
   The "child safety" concern surfaces a one-tap link to the safety page.
3. **Priority Ranking** — Sort 11 legal/custody topics (Custody, Possession
   Schedule, Special Education, Mental Health, Medication, Geographic
   Restriction, Right of First Refusal, Morality Clause, Telephonic Access,
   Airport Transportation, Other) plus any custom additions into four buckets:
   *Most urgent* / *Most emotionally difficult* / *Easier agreements* /
   *Willing to compromise*. Drag-and-drop. This becomes the agenda.
4. **Communication Style** — A 3-question self-assessment (avoider /
   escalator / defensive / over-explainer / passive / balanced) with an
   optional sample message that Claude can re-frame in a non-blaming, child-
   centered way.
5. **Readiness Check** — Six 1-to-5 self-ratings on listening, separating
   parenting from past hurt, staying calm, focusing on the future, and
   speaking respectfully. The score becomes a readiness label
   (*Needs Support* / *Moderately Ready* / *Prepared for Mediation*).

## What the parent gets out of it

Three AI-synthesized documents, all generated by **Claude Sonnet 4.5** via
Emergent's universal LLM key:

- **Mediation Summary** — A focused, mediator-ready PDF: top concerns,
  priority agenda, flexibility areas, communication goals, notes for the
  mediator, readiness score. Downloadable, shareable via 7-day signed link,
  and emailable directly to a mediator (with the parent's email as reply-to).
- **Co-Parenting Agreement Draft** — A neutral, *non-legal* starter document
  the parent can bring to the table: shared goals for the child,
  communication clauses, child-needs clauses, household-rules clauses,
  priority topics, and a closing note. Drafted to *invite conversation*,
  never to dictate.
- **Things I Can Improve On** — A personal growth plan compiled from the
  Communication and Readiness data. Each focus area pairs **3 specific
  communication tips** with **2 quality-of-life tips**, plus a
  *"Try this week"* list of 3 do-it-tomorrow actions. Designed to feel warm
  and specific — never preachy.

All three are stored, re-generatable, and history-aware (parents can flip
between past versions).

## Safety — the room with the dimmer switch

Every page has a one-tap **Safety** link. The Safety page includes:

- A "Quick Exit" button (and **ESC** key shortcut) that replaces the tab
  with a neutral page — for survivors browsing on a monitored device.
- National 24/7 hotlines (Domestic Violence, StrongHearts Native Helpline,
  love is respect, RAINN, **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**).
- San Antonio–specific resources (Family Violence Prevention Services,
  Bexar County Family Justice Center, PEACE Initiative).
- A full **Parallel Parenting framework** — explaining why traditional
  co-parenting can be unsafe in DV cases, the structural features of
  parallel parenting, six concern domains, clinical/legal considerations,
  and what a strong parenting plan includes.
- Privacy guidance for survivors (incognito mode, device monitoring, etc).

## Other features

- **Resource Center** — Eight curated, clickable resources (videos + a Bill
  Eddy book link) across Communication, High Conflict, Parallel Parenting,
  Rebuilding Trust, and Parenting After Divorce.
- **Live Facebook feed** embedded on the Resources page, plus footer/header
  links to the SA Coparents Facebook page.
- **Authentication** — JWT email/password and Google sign-in (via Emergent's
  managed OAuth flow). HttpOnly cookies, never localStorage.

## Design language

Muted earthy palette — **sage**, **sand**, **terracotta**, **cream** — with a
serif display font for warmth and quiet authority. The tone everywhere is
warm, non-judgmental, and unmistakably child-centered. The product feels
*calm* on purpose: this is not a SaaS app, it's a preparation ritual.
